Fries

Description

French Fries are the ultimate universal comfort food. Perfectly crispy on the outside and soft and fluffy on the inside, these golden batons of potato are deep-fried to perfection and seasoned simply with salt. While they are a staple side dish, achieving the perfect crunch requires a specific double-fry technique.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 Large Russet potatoes (best for starch content)
  • Vegetable oil for frying (Peanut or Canola works well)
  • Sea salt to taste
  • Optional: Black pepper or paprika for seasoning
  • Cold water (for soaking)

Steps

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into even strips, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
  2. Place the cut potatoes in a bowl of cold water and soak for at least 30 minutes to remove excess starch.
  3. Drain the potatoes and pat them completely dry with a clean kitchen towel.
  4. Heat oil in a heavy pot to $160$°C ($325$°F) and fry the potatoes in batches for about 3-5 minutes until pale and soft.
  5. Remove the fries and let them rest on a wire rack while you increase the oil heat to 190°C (375°F).
  6. Fry the potatoes a second time for 2-3 minutes until they are golden brown and crispy.
  7. Drain on paper towels and toss with salt immediately while still hot.
